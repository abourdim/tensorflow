AI Hacker Lab — Demo Guide



5-Minute Demo Script


Use this to demonstrate AI Hacker Lab at events, meetings, or community gatherings.



Opening (30 seconds)

"This is AI Hacker Lab — a free, open-source workshop series that teaches AI and Machine Learning to all ages. It runs in a browser. No installation, no accounts. Let me show you."

Open ai-hacker-lab-single.html and show the hub.



Workshop 1 Demo (90 seconds)

"For ages 6-9, we have Junior Hacker — a sorting game."

  • Click Workshop 1, enter a name, click Start Mission
  • Show intro screen: "Scientists sort things into groups. AI does the same."
  • Start Mission 1: drag 2-3 items to correct zones
  • Show the sound effects, encouragement messages, score counter
  • "Kids learn classification — the foundation of all AI."


    • Workshop 2 Demo (90 seconds)

    "Ages 10-13 get Code Breaker — they draw shapes and train a real neural network."

  • Open workshop-2.html separately
  • Show the briefing screens explaining neural networks
  • Draw a quick circle, save it
  • "They collect training data, then TensorFlow.js trains a CNN right in the browser."
  • "After training, they test it — draw a shape and the AI predicts what it is."


    • Workshop 4 Demo (60 seconds)

    "Parents and teachers get AI Decoded — so they understand what their kids are learning."

  • Show the chapter navigation
  • Click "What is AI?" — show the interactive sorting demo
  • Click "Ethics" — show the discussion scenario
  • "It's not just for kids. Parents need AI literacy too."


    • Bonus Tools (30 seconds)

    "Plus: certificates they can print, a progress dashboard, a learning journal, and a time capsule where kids predict AI's future."

    Show certificates page, generate a quick certificate.



    Closing (30 seconds)

    "Everything is free, open source, and runs offline. It's designed by Muslims, for everyone. The Bismillah is at the top of every page. No music, culturally respectful, and scientifically rigorous."
    "Download it, host it, share it. Workshop-diy.org."


    Live Demo Checklist


  • [ ] Chrome or Edge browser installed
  • [ ] All HTML files in same folder
  • [ ] Internet available (for Workshop 2-3 TF.js)
  • [ ] Projector or shared screen ready
  • [ ] Sound on (for effect) or off (for quiet venues)
  • [ ] Test all workshops before the demo
  • [ ] Have a backup plan if internet fails (Workshop 1 works offline)
