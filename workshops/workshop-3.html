AI Mistakes Gallery

When AI Goes Wrong in the Real World

These aren't just funny — they have real consequences

⚖️
Biased Hiring Algorithm
A major tech company's AI hiring tool was found to discriminate against women because it was trained on 10 years of resumes — mostly from men.
Why? → Historical bias in training data gets amplified by AI
Lesson: Biased data → biased AI. Fairness must be designed in.
🏥
Skin Tone Accuracy Gap
A medical AI for detecting skin conditions was significantly less accurate on darker skin tones because training images were predominantly from lighter-skinned patients.
Why? → Underrepresentation in training data = unequal performance
Lesson: AI must work equally well for everyone — representation matters.
🌐
Gendered Translations
Translation AI would translate "doctor" as "he" and "nurse" as "she" in languages with gendered pronouns — reflecting societal stereotypes.
Why? → Language data contains human biases that AI learns and reproduces
Lesson: AI mirrors the data it's given — including our biases.
🚗
Self-Driving Edge Cases
Autonomous vehicles have struggled with unusual scenarios: a cyclist carrying a bicycle, a truck with reflective surfaces, or pedestrians in unexpected positions.
Why? → Edge cases are rare in training data but critical in the real world
Lesson: Real-world AI must handle situations it's never seen before.
