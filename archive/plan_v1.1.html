Workshop 1: "Junior Hacker: Sort Mission"
Ages 6–9

Topic: Inventions & Machines — One main topic with increasing difficulty across 3 missions

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what classification means
  • Learn that AI sorts things by looking at features (type, power, era)
  • Practice pattern recognition
  • Discover that AI faces tricky decisions too

Screens & Flow

01
Welcome
Bismillah top-centered. "Welcome, Junior Hacker!" title. Name input (placeholder: "Yusuf"). Animated science lab background (CSS). Big "Start Mission" button.
02
What is a Scientist?
Step 1 of 3 intro cards. "Scientists look at things carefully and sort them into groups." Visual: 3 groups (robots, vehicles, gadgets) with emoji icons.
03
What is AI?
Step 2 of 3. "AI is a computer that learns to sort things — just like you!" Animation showing items moving into boxes.
04
Your Mission
Step 3 of 3. "Your mission: Help the AI learn by sorting inventions correctly." "Start Mission 1" button.
05
Mission 1: Sort by Type (Easy)
Categories: Robots 🤖 | Vehicles 🚗 | Gadgets 📱. 9 items to drag & drop. Score counter. Encouragement messages.
  • Robots: 🤖 robot, 🦾 robot arm, 🤖 drone bot
  • Vehicles: 🚗 car, 🚀 rocket, 🚁 helicopter
  • Gadgets: 📱 phone, 💻 laptop, ⌚ smartwatch
06
Mission 2: Sort by Power Source (Medium)
Categories: Electric ⚡ | Fuel 🔥 | Human-Powered 💪. 12 items, trickier decisions. Discussion: "Some machines use MORE THAN ONE power source — AI faces tricky decisions too!"
  • Electric: electric car, laptop, smartwatch, drone
  • Fuel: rocket, airplane, motorcycle, submarine
  • Human-powered: bicycle, skateboard, sailboat, hand drill
07
Mission 3: Sort by Era (Hard)
Categories: Ancient 🏛️ | Modern 🏙️ | Future 🚀. 12 items, requires critical thinking. Discussion: "You used FEATURES — that's exactly how AI thinks!"
  • Ancient: wheel, compass, sundial, catapult
  • Modern: smartphone, car, television, microwave
  • Future: flying car, robot helper, space elevator, teleporter
08
Hacker Badge & Summary
"Mission Complete!" animated badge. Stats: 33 inventions, 9 categories. Learning: "TYPE, POWER, and TIME are FEATURES. AI uses features to classify millions of things!" Badge: "{name} — Junior Machine Hacker"

  • See how a neural network learns from examples
  • Experience the train → test → improve cycle
  • Understand accuracy and why more data = better AI

Workshop 3: "AI Engineer: Build & Deploy"
Ages 14+

Topic: MNIST Digit Recognition — Real TensorFlow.js

Learning Objectives

  • Understand neural network architecture (layers, neurons, activation)
  • Write/modify real TensorFlow.js code
  • Train on a real dataset (MNIST digits)
  • Understand loss, epochs, batch size, overfitting
  • Experiment with hyperparameters

Screens & Flow (9 screens)

01
Welcome
Clean dev environment. "Welcome, AI Engineer." Name input (placeholder: "Ibrahim"). "Open Workspace" button.
02
Module 1: What's Inside a Neural Network?
Interactive network visualizer. Add/remove neurons and layers. Sliders for layers (1-5) and neurons (2-32). Animated data flow.
03
Module 2: Meet the MNIST Dataset
Grid of handwritten digits from TensorFlow.js. Click to see label + pixel values. "60k training, 10k testing — why split?"
04
Module 3: Build Your Model (Code Editor)
Split screen: editable code (left) + visualization (right). Pre-filled starter code. "Build Model" updates architecture diagram.
05
Module 4: Train & Watch
Hyperparameter controls (epochs, batch size, learning rate). Real-time loss & accuracy charts. Live predictions updating. 30-60 seconds.
06
Module 5: Test & Analyze
Test accuracy. Confusion matrix. Most/least confident predictions. Draw your own digit → model predicts.
07
Module 6: Experiments & Challenges
5 challenges: >95% accuracy, 1 epoch only, high learning rate, extra layer, limited data. Hypothesis → experiment → result. Leaderboard.
08
Module 7: The Ethics Lab
Interactive scenarios on bias, fairness, responsibility. Universal ethics + optional Islamic reflections section.

AI Engineer Badge & Summary
Badge, stats, concepts summary. Next steps: TensorFlow tutorials, Kaggle. "{name} — Certified AI Engineer"

Tools of the Trade — tools-of-the-trade.html
All Ages

A dedicated standalone page covering TensorFlow and alternatives. Linked from every workshop via a "Tools of the Trade 🔧" button. Never interrupts the mission/game flow.

Purpose

  • Introduce TensorFlow and explain it at every age level
  • Present alternatives so learners understand the landscape
  • Keep tool education separate from the hands-on workshop experience
  • Each workshop links to its age-appropriate tab within this page

Tab 1: "For Young Hackers" (Ages 6-9)

TF
What is TensorFlow?
"TensorFlow is a tool that helps computers learn — like a brain for machines! Scientists at Google built it." Friendly visual of a computer with a brain glowing inside. Analogy: "A calculator helps you do math. TensorFlow helps computers learn."
ALT
Other Cool Tools
Scratch + Machine Learning — drag-and-drop blocks. Teachable Machine — train with your camera, no code. ml5.js — friendly helper for beginners.
💡
Big Idea
"There are many tools for teaching computers. TensorFlow is one of the most popular — and you're learning it!"

Tab 2: "For Code Breakers" (Ages 10-13)

TF
What is TensorFlow?
"TensorFlow is an open-source machine learning library created by Google. TensorFlow.js is the version that runs in your browser. It's the engine that powers the AI you just trained." Diagram: your drawing → TensorFlow.js → prediction.
ALT
The AI Toolkit
Comparison cards for: TensorFlow.js (Google, what we use), PyTorch (Meta, popular with researchers), ml5.js (beginner-friendly), Teachable Machine (no-code), Scratch + ML (AI-powered games). Each card: name, creator, difficulty, best for.
💡
Big Idea
"TensorFlow is powerful and versatile — but it's not the only option. Good engineers know multiple tools."

Tab 3: "For AI Engineers" (Ages 14+)

TF
What is TensorFlow?
"Google's open-source framework for ML and deep learning. Released 2015, one of the most widely-used ML frameworks." History timeline: Google Brain → TF 1.0 (2017) → TF 2.0 (2019) → TF.js.
ALT
The Full Landscape
Full comparison table: TensorFlow/TF.js (Google, Python/JS, production), PyTorch (Meta, Python, research), scikit-learn (community, classic ML), Keras (fast prototyping), ONNX (interoperability), Hugging Face (pre-trained models), JAX (high-performance research). Includes difficulty levels and use cases.
</>
Code Comparison
"Hello World" side-by-side in TensorFlow.js, PyTorch, and scikit-learn. Same task, different syntax. Shows transferable concepts.
🧭
When to Use What
Decision guide: Web → TF.js. Research → PyTorch. Prototype → Keras. Not deep learning → scikit-learn. Pre-trained models → Hugging Face.
💡
Big Idea
"The concepts you learn — layers, training, loss, accuracy — work across ALL these tools. You're learning transferable skills."

Tab 4: "For Parents & Teachers"

TF
What is TensorFlow?
"Google's free, open-source AI tool. Used by Airbnb, Twitter, Intel, and thousands of startups. Your kids are learning the same tool used by real engineers. TF.js means no installation — just open and learn."
❓
Why TensorFlow for These Workshops?
Free and open-source. Runs in the browser (no setup). Industry-standard (real career skills). Huge community. Works on any device.
ALT
Other Tools Your Kids Might Encounter
PyTorch (the other major framework), Scratch + ML (for under 10s), Teachable Machine (no-code), Hugging Face (pre-trained models), scikit-learn (traditional ML).
💡
Key Message
"The specific tool doesn't matter as much as the concepts. Classification, training, features, accuracy — these ideas are universal. Your child is learning to think like an AI engineer, not just use one tool."
